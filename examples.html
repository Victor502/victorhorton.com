AshleysCloset.store

This was a project i did for a customer that wants to sell the clothing she no longer wears. The though behind the website was so she could reach more people than at a yard sales. She also wanted to use different social media sources so she could showcase clothing to people and have a place were they could see all of the clothing she had available. This website uses Pay-pal's cart and checkout feature. This allowed the customer to track the inventory and manage shipping. This site was developed with a few JQuery plug-in's, HTML5, CSS3 and Javascript.

LouisvilleSmartHome

This was a project i did for my CodeLouisville course. The theory behind it is to reach people that want to bring their home into the "smart home" revolution. I used HTML5, CSS3 and JavaScript. I used Zurb’s Foundations framework as a starting point and added a JQuery plug-in for some interaction. I made a contact page which included some PHP and a form validation to make sure all of the fields are filled out before allowing it to be sent.
