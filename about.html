Thank you for wanting to learn more.

I graduated from the University of Louisville in 2004 with a bachelors degree in communications. I took a few website development classes while there, but nothing in depth. My experience in the past has been with drag and drop applications writing just a little HTML and CSS. I developed my first website for a festival I put together in 2005. It was what the industry refers to as a static website or an informational website. Nothing fancy but it got the job done. I went on to make several websites for friends and family members.

I've been studying HTML5, CSS3 and JavaScript for just over a year. I recently graduated from a program called CodeLouisville where I studied frontend development. I got some in depth training with HTML5, CSS3 and JavaScript. I also got the opportunity to use some popular frameworks which include Twitter BootStrap, Zurbs Foundations and JQuery. I took the opportunity to get some experience using JQuery plug-in's which are used to give a website great interaction. And I’m proud to say I’m comfortable using the popular repository GIT.

I am currently back at CodeLouisville where I am learning full-stack JavaScript which will include server side interaction.
